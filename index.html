This presentation will tell it to you:

  • Land use drives Mozambique's carbon emissions, climate risk, and low income.
  • Most land is not cultivated.
  • Cultivated land is not productive.
  • Uncultivated land is deforested for household fuel, especially near cities.
  • The PP Framework will help us understand why.

Land use drives Mozambique's emissions.

Mozambican incomes are land-use driven.

Agriculture employs 90% of women and
        70% of men in Mozambique.
It represents 24% of the country's GDP.

Climate risk is serious in Mozambique.

Mozambique is among the world's most at risk countries for droughts and flooding from climate change. Subsistence agriculture dependence means droughts will lead to starvation as well as poverty.

How Mozambique uses land:

Even on cultivated land, productivity is low.

Uncultivated land is deforested for fuel.

Each year, 529 square miles of land are deforested for fuel.
50% of the country remains forested
The forest is shrinking 0.5-1.5% each year
